✍️ History of Drawing – From Caves to Creativity

🪨Prehistoric Era (30,000–10,000 BCE)

The earliest drawings are found on cave walls in places like Lascaux (France) and Altamira (Spain).
These drawings, made with charcoal and natural pigments, depicted animals, humans, and hunting scenes.
Drawing was used for communication, rituals, and storytelling before writing existed.

🏛️ Ancient Civilizations

In Egypt, drawings were used in hieroglyphs and tomb paintings to tell stories of gods and pharaohs.
In Greece and Rome, drawing helped develop proportions and anatomy. Artists used it to plan sculptures and architecture.

✝️ Medieval Period (5th–15th Century)

Art was mostly religious—drawings served as illustrations in manuscripts and stained-glass designs.
Artists used ink, gold leaf, and natural dyes in their works.

🎨 Renaissance (14th–17th Century) – The Golden Age

Drawing became a respected art form and foundational skill. Masters like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael used drawing to explore science, anatomy, and architecture.
Paper became more accessible, and sketchbooks were used.

🖌️ 17th–19th Century

Drawing evolved with styles like Baroque, Romanticism, and Realism.
Artists used it for both preliminary sketches and as finished artworks. Tools expanded: charcoal, graphite, pen & ink, pastels.

🖥️ 20th Century to Now

Modernism brought abstract and expressive styles (think Picasso, Kandinsky, Matisse).
Today, drawing includes digital art, animation, and 3D design. Still used in fashion design, architecture, game design, and fine art.
